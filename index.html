Meeting with Punjab Health Minister

Dr. Ravinder Mohan and Dr. Harbans Singh Sran from the Patiala centre, met Punjab’s Minister of Health & Family Welfare, Dr. Balbir Singh, to discuss strengthening palliative care services in the state. The Hon'ble Minister emphasised the government’s commitment to ensuring quality and comprehensive healthcare for patients with serious and terminal illnesses.

CanSupport at India International Trade Fair

CanSupport participated in the Health Pavilion at the India International Trade Fair (IITF) 2025 in collaboration with the National Programme for Palliative Care (NCPC), Ministry of Health and Family Welfare at Pragati Maidan, New Delhi. The team focused on raising awareness about palliative care and CanSupport’s services. Staff members engaged with visitors from diverse backgrounds, highlighting the role of palliative care in pain management and end-of-life support.

Memorandum Signed with IOCL

CanSupport signed a Memorandum of Agreement with Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. (IOCL) under its CSR initiative to support the continued expansion of our reach in Punjab and enhance community-based palliative care services. The partnership will help strengthen outreach and improve quality of life for patients and families in need across the region.

VOLUNTEER FEATURE

Neeta Bhalla began her journey with CanSupport in 2018 after learning about the organisation through her house help. Deeply moved by the impact of the work being done, she reached out to contribute in any way she could. Neeta soon became actively involved in weekday sessions, working closely with women participants and creating handmade bags that helped raise funds for them and other patients and caregivers. Save for the break due to the pandemic, Neeta eagerly resumed her association and got back to supporting day care sessions.

Livelihood Support

A cancer diagnosis can suddenly take away a patient’s ability to work - and with it, their family’s only source of income. For many of the people CanSupport serves, this means losing not just financial stability, but also independence and dignity. With your support, we can continue to help patients procure pushcarts and sewing machines which enable them to restart small livelihoods and get enabled to earn for their families again.
